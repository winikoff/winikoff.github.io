Michael Winikoff's Medleys

This page is an attempt to collate and capture my revue items.

I am hoping to be able to get recordings (e.g. for 50IV) or create recordings. Now that I have a MIDI keyboard I've started experimenting with recording MIDIs which works reasonably well. What's more fun is orchestrating the revue items - expect to see more of these!

I often prepare (or complete) my revue items at the last minute during the festival. This is intentional so I can include memorable themes from that festival. The performance is usually done from a short list of themes with additional notes (e.g. keys, reminders about transitions). In recent years (starting with Nyevsky) I've started to prepare more sophisticated scores. In the case of Harry Potter the performance was from brief notes augmented with handwritten music paper. The nice PDF was written after the festival.

Somewhat oddly, I have better records for stuff that I did 10 years than for stuff that I did 3 years ago ... go figure. (update: ironically, when updating this page I realised I didn't have the final version of the Brisbane 2007 revue item!)

I am happy for members of the AICSA/IV community to copy any of the material off this page, including MP3 files, as long as suitable attribution is retained.

Last, but certainly not least, I'd like to thank Rod Reynolds for his work in recording the IV revues, and for his permission to make the recordings available.

2006

At my third(!) Adelaide IV I changed tack slightly, and instead of performing a new piano piece, I instead "arranged" a choral piece: Fanfare for the Common Chorister. Based, as the name suggests, on Copland's Fanfare for the Common Man, this piece also featured a number of "guest appearances" by John Williams, Howard Shore, Carl Orff and others. It was performed by the Convergents, consisting of Bron, David, Emma, Kate, Leanne, Mark, myself, Michelle, Philip, Sandra, and Woody; and won the award for the best revue item. (PDF, and another PDF but without the cover page and smaller; also MIDI and MP3)

I also performed Rhapsody in Red. The performance was dedicated to Bruce Alcorn. Way back in 1994, after I had performed To Boldly Go, Bruce said something along the lines of "but you left out Red Dwarf". This was the impetus for Rhapsody in Red which was first performed later that year, at the 1994 Minifest in Melbourne. Although Rhapsody in Red had been performed a number of times since then, Bruce stopped attending IVs ... until 2006, which was the first time he heard the piece (MP3)

At some point after hearing Rhapsody in Red Bruce did comment that I should look at FireFly ... hmmm ... stay tuned ... :-)
